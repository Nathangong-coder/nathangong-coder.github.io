Why should you learn and play bridge?

1. Those who play more bridge frequently score higher on cognitive tests.

2. Bridge games offer intellectual and social stimulation on a routine basis.

3. An area in the brain used in playing bridge stimulates the immune system. Researchers suggest that is because players must use memory, visualization and sequencing.

4. Any activity from which you derive pleasure prevents the onset of classic depression. Players are less likely to be depressed; hence, they sleep better, tend to exercise more and have a better life in general.

5. Multiple changes in lifestyle (e.g. cognitive training and social activity, two of bridge’s chief benefits) can improve memory and thinking in those at risk for cognitive decline.

6. Playing bridge can help any person retain their mental sharpness.

7. The frequency of playing games is associated with greater brain volume in several regions that are affected by Alzheimer’s disease.

8. Successful people play bridge! Warren Buffet and Bill Gates are considered to be two of the smartest entrepreneurs in America. Both are also very accomplished bridge players.
